# Quote Selection Examples

## Good Quotes

1. "The best way to have good ideas is to have lots of ideas and throw away the bad ones."
   WHY GOOD: Complete idea, surprising framing, stands alone.

2. "Dopamine is not about pleasure. It's about the anticipation of pleasure."
   WHY GOOD: Corrects a misconception precisely, one sentence.

3. "Every system is perfectly designed to get the results it gets."
   WHY GOOD: Profound reframe, universally applicable, memorable.

4. "The biggest risk is not taking any risk. In a world that's changing fast, the only strategy guaranteed to fail is not taking risks."
   WHY GOOD: Bold claim, specific enough, complete thought.

## Bad Quotes (Do NOT Select These)

5. "Yeah, I mean, it's like, you know what I mean?"
   WHY BAD: Filler speech, no content.

6. "That's a really great question."
   WHY BAD: Conversational noise, not an idea.

7. "So when I was at Stanford in 2015 and we were working on this project with the team and we had just gotten funding and there was this moment where everything clicked and I realized that the way we were thinking about neural networks was fundamentally wrong because..."
   WHY BAD: Way too long, no clear endpoint, needs context.

8. "You should really try meditation."
   WHY BAD: Generic advice, not insightful or surprising.

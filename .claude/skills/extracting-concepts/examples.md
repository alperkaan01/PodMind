# Concept Extraction Examples

## Good Extractions

1. **"dopamine scheduling"** — Deliberately timing dopamine-triggering activities
   to maintain motivation without building tolerance.
   WHY GOOD: Named, specific, transferable to other contexts.

2. **"neuroplasticity"** — The brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming
   new neural connections throughout life.
   WHY GOOD: Named scientific concept with broad explanatory power.

3. **"probabilistic thinking"** — Making decisions by assigning probabilities to
   outcomes rather than binary yes/no predictions.
   WHY GOOD: Named framework applicable across domains.

4. **"build in public"** — Sharing your creation process openly to build
   audience and get feedback simultaneously.
   WHY GOOD: Named movement/strategy with specific meaning.

5. **"second-order effects"** — Consequences of consequences; the indirect
   results of a decision beyond its immediate impact.
   WHY GOOD: Named thinking tool with broad applicability.

## Bad Extractions (Do NOT Extract These)

6. **"John's morning routine"** — A specific person's habit sequence.
   WHY BAD: Episode-specific anecdote, not transferable.

7. **"be more disciplined"** — Generic self-help advice.
   WHY BAD: No specific framework, just vague advice.

8. **"OpenAI"** — A company name.
   WHY BAD: Proper noun without conceptual weight.

9. **"technology is advancing"** — Observable trend without insight.
   WHY BAD: Too vague to be useful as a concept node.

10. **"the interview was great"** — Meta-commentary about the episode.
    WHY BAD: Not a concept at all, just filler.
